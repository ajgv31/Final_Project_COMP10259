FUN FACTS
Specie Name: Atlantic Goliath Grouper also known as Jewfish
Weight: Up to 800 lbs
Height: Up to 8 feet
Lifespan: More than 30 years
Food Habit: Crustaceans, fishes, octopus, and young sea turtles
Predators: Large fishes like barracuda, king mackerel and other groupers, sandbar shark, and hammerhead shark
Threats: Overfishing, habitat loss
Habitat: Western Atlantic Ocean from Florida to Brazil, the Caribbean and the Gulf of Mexico
Range: Rocky, coral and mud bottoms in marine waters up to 150 feet.


Is it possible to consume Goliath Grouper?

This species has been granted protection status under the law. The Florida Fish and Wildlife Conservation Commission (FWC) forbids the collection or retention of Goliath Grouper. In areas where they were caught, 40% of the Goliath Grouper caught had mercury levels which exceeded those recommended in the United States as safe for consumption by humans. So, it is not safe to eat this fish.
